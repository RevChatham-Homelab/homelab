# Purpose

This document defines the engineering philosophy that guides the design, implementation, documentation, and long-term maintenance of the RevChatham Homelab.

These philosophies provide the foundation for technical decisions and project governance.

The Engineering Principles document describes how these philosophies are applied during day-to-day engineering work.

For engineering execution and operational practices, see:

- ENGINEERING_PRINCIPLES.md

Where uncertainty exists, these philosophies should guide engineering judgment.

---

# Project Before Ego

Engineering decisions shall always be made in the best interest of the project.

Personal preference, previous decisions, or attachment to an existing implementation shall never prevent adopting a demonstrably better solution.

The project is improved through evidence, not ownership.

---

# Use First. Standardize Second.

Practical experience is preferred over premature standardization.

Solutions should first demonstrate operational value before becoming project standards.

Standards are created from successful implementation, not assumptions.

---

# Unknown Information Shall Remain Unknown Until Verified

Repository documentation shall distinguish verified information from assumptions.

Unknown information shall never be fabricated, estimated, or inferred simply to complete documentation.

Accuracy is always preferred over completeness.

---

# Integrity Over Convenience

Repository documentation shall accurately reflect the current state of the project.

Information shall never be altered solely to improve presentation or appearance.

Documentation exists to describe reality, not to create it.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Engineering decisions may be revised whenever better information, improved practices, or operational experience demonstrate a superior solution.

Previous decisions are respected but are never immune from improvement.

---

# Verification Before Completion

Work is not considered complete until it has been verified.

Verification may include technical validation, peer review, operational testing, repository audits, or documented recovery testing.

Completion requires evidence.

---

# Documentation as Infrastructure

Documentation is considered part of the engineering deliverable.

Infrastructure is not complete until the corresponding documentation accurately reflects its implementation.

Documentation shall be maintained alongside the infrastructure it describes.

---

# Standards Before Scale

Reusable standards should be established before they are widely adopted throughout the project whenever practical.

Well-defined standards improve consistency, maintainability, and long-term project quality.

---

# Build for Today. Prepare for Tomorrow.

Future expansion should be considered during engineering decisions without allowing speculative requirements to delay current objectives.

The project prioritizes completing today's milestone while remaining adaptable to future growth.

Future-proofing shall support the current project rather than replace it.

---

# Engineering Through Evidence

Recommendations should be supported by operational experience, testing, documentation, or established engineering practices.

Technical decisions should be based upon evidence rather than preference.

---

# Living Philosophy

This document is expected to evolve as the RevChatham Homelab matures.

New philosophies may be added when they represent enduring engineering principles rather than temporary project decisions.
